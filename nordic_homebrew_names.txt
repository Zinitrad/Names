                                                        CLAN NAMES
 
A note on the names of nordic clans
 
nordic clan names come in three forms.  
 
There's dithemic form, in which two words are combined into a 'phrase' that carries meaning. This is pretty much how ancient germanic naming is structured, and its likely the way the names are is simply due to modern in world convention of translating them.  Its likely ancient nord clan names were in nordic language.
 
Then there's patronymic form.  Patronymic form is not common among nords, and has only had one or two instances in the normal games, 4 in ESO, and was prevalent in Arena.  Its rare, but it exists.  For Patronymic form, simply make a male name, and make it into a name ending in -son, -sen, -sdottir, -sdohtr, etc.  You may have to add vowels, and change or remove consonants as neccessary.  These are usually meant as an indicator of descendence from a hero or important figure
 
Finally, there's untranslated form.  This form is extremely rare, and only appeared a few times in Skyrim so far.  For this form, just generate a male name.
